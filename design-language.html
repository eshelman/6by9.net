Data Visualization Principles (Tufte)

High Data-Ink Ratio

Maximize the share of ink devoted to data. Remove all non-essential decoration, gridlines, and embellishments.

Avoid Chartjunk

No 3D effects, gratuitous gradients, or visual clutter. Every pixel should earn its place.

Small Multiples

Repeat a similar graphic structure to show change across conditions. Enables pattern recognition.

Layering & Separation

Use subtle visual hierarchy to distinguish data from labels and axes without competing for attention.

Integrate Text & Graphics

Labels and annotations should live within the visualization, not in separate legends when possible.

Show Data Variation

Don't lie with scales. Start axes at zero when appropriate. Show honest uncertainty.
